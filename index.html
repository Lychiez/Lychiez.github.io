6 Easy Steps: How to Solve
a 3x3 Rubik's Cube

Author: Anthony Chuang



Introduction

Solving a 3x3 Rubik's Cube isn't as hard as it looks. In fact, here at Crystal Solve, anyone can learn to solve a cube with 6 simple steps. However, its helpful to first become familiar with cube notation in order to quickly learn certain algorithms. But don't worry! There are only a few algorithms needed for this method, dubbed the "crystal method".



Pieces

There are three different pieces that make up the cube:

  1. Center piece
  2. Edge piece
  3. Corner piece

Center pieces cannot be moved, and the color determines the side. There are 6 center pieces.

Edge pieces can be moved, and two sides share the same center piece. There are 12 edge pieces.

Corner pieces can be moved, and 3 sides share the same corner piece. There are 8 corner pieces



Notation

There are different turns or actions you can do with a cube, and these actions are compiled together called notations.

  • U: rotate the up face of the cube clockwise
  • D: rotate the down face of the cube clockwise
  • R: rotate the right face of the cube clockwise
  • L: rotate the left face of the cube clockwise
  • F: rotate the front face of the cube clockwise
  • B: rotate the back face of the cube clockwise
  • ': rotate it counterclockwise instead of clockwise
  • 2: rotate it two times

    • SolveTheCube is a great site for learning notation.



    Solving a 3x3

    Step 1: White Cross

    To solve the white cross, you must first find the white center piece, and align all the edge pieces on the white side. This step is intuitive, and after practicing, you can cut lots of time on this step.

    Step 2: Solving the first layer

    To solve finish the first layer, you just have to slide the white corners into place. First align the edge in the bottom layer so that either side is matched with its corresponding center piece. Then, if the corner piece is on the right side, do the first algorithm, and if its on the left, do the second algorithm.

    Algorithms Needed:
    DR'D'R
    D'LDL'

    Step 3: Solving the second layer

    To solve the second layer, you just have to align the edge pieces in the second layer. Start by flipping the cube upside down so the yellow side is facing up (remember the center piece color determines the side), and the white side is facing down. Now set up a chosen edge piece. It is best to first do all the ones on the top rather than chose the edge pieces in the second layer. So first, align the side color with its corresponding center piece. Now, there are two different cases. If the top color is to the right of the aligned piece, do the first algorithm. If it's to the left, do the second algorithm. However, if the piece is already in the second layer but oriented incorrectly, then do either the first or second algorithm (which would move the top piece out), then perform the required algorithm to slide it back in.

    Algorithms Needed:
    URU'R'U'F'UF
    U'F'UFURU'R'

    Step 4: Solving the yellow cross

    To solve the yellow cross, you need to align the edge pieces. To do this, you only need to know one algorithm, but there are 3 different unsolved cases. *In this step, ignore the corner pieces.*

    Yellow Dot: If you have a yellow dot, do the algorithm once then go to the yellow L step

    Yellow L: If you have a yellow L, manipulate the cube so that the L is in the top left corner. Next, do the algorithm once, and then move onto the Yellow Line step.

    Yellow line: If you have a yellow line, do the algorithm once to get a yellow cross

    Algorithms Needed:
    FRUR'U'F'

    Step 5: Solving the yellow side

    To solve the yellow side, you need to align the corner pieces. You only need one algorithm to solve the yellow side. Start by manuevering the top so the bottom left corner is not yellow. Then do teh algorithm. As soon as the corner is yellow on top, do U' and continue to do the algorithm over and over again. Repeat until finished. *Ignore the displacement of the first two layers. The pieces will rearrange themselves by the time you finish this step*

    Algorithms Needed:
    R'DRD'--repeat until corner is yellow

    Step 6: Solving the Cube

    To solve the last layer, you need to identify certain cases, and utilze the corresponding algorithms.

    All different colors on all sides: first algorithm

    2 edge pieces with the same color on the same side (one side only): Hold the cube so the 2 edge pieces are algined with their centerpiece, and then do the first algorithm.

    All three pieces the same color on the same side (one side only): Align the top layer with matching centerpiece, then do first algorithm.

    Three pieces with the same color on one side only, and 3 other sides have 2 matching edges:

    Edge piece to the right: second algorithm

    Edge piece to the left: third algorithm

    Algorithms Needed:
    R'FR'2B'RF'R'2B'2RU'
    2FU'LR'2FL'RU'2F
    2FULR'2FL'RU2F



    Congratulations, you just solved a Rubik's Cube!
